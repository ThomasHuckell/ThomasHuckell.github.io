QMAST

I started working for QMAST as a general member of the mechanical design team where I was trained in a range of rapid prototyping methods such as 3D printing, laser cutting, and CNC milling in addition to learning the fabrication process of composite materials. After a year as a general member I was promoted to manager of the mechanical team. In this role I was in charge of planning, precurement of materials, training and workload delegation. Additionally, as manager of the mechanical team I leasied with the electical and software teams to coordinate deadlines and dependecies between teams.

Project Overview

One of my main contribution was designing and fabricating the sailboat's hull. A time lapse of the design progress can be seen in the image above. The process started with designing a CAD model of the hull, This model was recreated to make a plug for to make the mold of the hull. This process consisted of taking cross sections of CAD model and using them to generate profiles to cut with a laser cutter. With cross sections profiles we could fill and fair the space between with foam. When the plug was finished it was prepped for a fiberglass layup to create a mold for the hull. The mold was then used to create the hulls shell which was fabricated with a carbon fiber layup. Bulkheads were finally added for stuctural support for electical and mechanical systems.
