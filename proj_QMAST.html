QMAST

I started working for QMAST as a general member of the mechanical design team where I was trained in a range of rapid prototyping methods such as 3D printing, laser cutting, and CNC milling in addition to learning the fabrication process of composite materials. After a year as a general member, I was promoted to manager of the mechanical team. In this role I oversaw planning, procurement of materials, training and workload delegation. Additionally, as manager of the mechanical team I liaised with the electrical and software teams to coordinate deadlines and dependencies between teams.

Project Overview

One of the projects I headed while on QMAST was redesigning the hull. Our goal was to improve the speed of the boat through reducing the hulls width and extending the keel length. The process started with designing a CAD model of the hull which was based upon a traditional bulb keel sailboat hull. This model was used to create a plug for the hull's mold. First, cross sections of the CAD model were used to generate profiles to cut with a laser cutter. These cut profiles are positioned at the appropriate distances using a support frame with the voids between profiles filled with foam then faired to match the desired contours. The plug was then prepped for a fiberglass layup to create a mold for the hull. The mold was then used to create the hulls shell which was fabricated with carbon fiber and reinforced with bulkheads for added structural support and to mount electro-mechanical systems.
