Improved standing balance of legged robots through leveraging unified reduced order model of standing model

Read My Thesis
Publications

Thesis Overview

Legged robots are complex systems with a high degree of freedom which make optimal control methodolgies for the whole body dynamics computationally complex, especially for planning over long time horizions. Reduced order models encapsulate the whole body dynamics into lower degree of freedom representation more suitible.

My research extended the existing reduced order models to include all availible standing balance strategies:

    ankle strategy: modulate center of pressure within the base of support.
    hip strategy: generate angular momentum about center of mass to produce restoring shear force at base of support.
    variable height strategy: use verticle acceleration of center of mass to momentarly increase ground reaction force magnitude.

With this unified model we examined how access to all balancing strategies impacts push recovery performance. The key contributions of my research was examining the stability regions of existing models to examine the contribution each balancing stratey has on overall stability, push recovery simulation using trajectory optimization of the unified model against existing models, and finally developed a nonlinear model predictive controller based on the unified and existing reduced order models then examining each controllers performance on push recovery simulations of the reduced order model dynamics as well as being implemented on simple simulated one legged robot.

Stability Analysis

For the stability analysis we examined the linear inverted pendumlum (LIP), linear inverted pendulum plus flywheel (LIPFW), and variable height inverted pendulum (VHIP) models. These models correspond to an ankle, ankle + hip, and ankle + variable height balancing strategy. We use athropromorphic model parameters so as to gain insight on human balancing and better elucidate each strategies utility in standing balance for legged robots.
