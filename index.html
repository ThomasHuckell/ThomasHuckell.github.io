About Me Hardworking and determined individual with a passion for robotics. I enjoy working hands on to design and develop solutions to tackle complex problems.

Get to know me!

I'm a recent MASc graduate in robotics currently living in Vancouver looking to start my career in the robotics industry.

I completed my BASc degree in Applied Mathematics and Mechanical Engineering at Queen's University. This program developed a strong theoretical background in modern controls and modeling of robotic systems.

I completed my MASc degree at Queen's University under the supervision of Amy Wu at Queen's University. My research utilizes optimal control methodologies and reduced order models of legged systems to improve standing balance.

Outside of work I enjoy being outdoors! I am an avid paddler who enjoys kayaking and stand up paddleboarding. I also have a passion for woodworking , specifically designing and building furniture.

Dynamic Parameter Identification of CRS A465 Manipulator

February-April 2020

In advanced robotic applications an accurate dynamic model is critical for designing control systems, planning operations, process supervision, simulations, and training. The dynamic parameters were experimentally identified for a CRS manipulator in the final project of a graduate controls course.

Kalman Filter for smartphone IMU

Nov - Dec 2019

In the final project of a graduate level course on Energetics & Mechanics of Locomotion, a Kalman filter for a smartphone's Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU) sensor was designed to make accurate measurements of motion more accessible.
