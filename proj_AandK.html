A&K Robotics

A&K Robotics is a start up with the aim to revolutionize how people navigate large public spaces with an AI-enabled micro mobility pod Cruz. We are currently working with multiple airports like YVR to deploy the Cruz mobility pods in dynamic environments like airport terminals to assist those with mobility challenges get to their gates independently.

Project Overview

At A&K robotics I am a Hardware Engineer. In this role I am responsible for research and development, and the design and fabrication of the mechanical and electrical systems of the robots we build.

The main focus of my work at A&K robotics has been designing and building the Cruz mobility pod. This platform was custom built from the ground up to integrate with a suite of sensors, CPUs and Drive system that enable the pod to navigate autonomously.

During this project I was responsible for:

  • Designing a steel chassis to integrate the drive system, external shell and sensors. This design was iterated upon and validated using static loading simulations using Finite Element Analysis.
  • Designing the Pods plastic external shell. After finishing the design I then worked with overseas manufacturers to achieve desired tolerance, finish and light diffusion.
  • Designing and sourcing and fabricating the electrical power distribution and the Communication networks (CAN,Ethernet,USB,I2C).
  • Fabricated custom components using 3D printers, water jet cutter, CNC, and press brake.
  • Developed production instructions for assembling the pod, fabricating it custom sub-assemblies and cables.

The dynamic work environment of a start-up has required me to fill a wide range of roles. I have had to take on a project management role with the hardware design, managing the hardware team's build schedule & purchasing to meet delivery targets. I am also involved with the site operations, maintenance, debugging and system diagnostics. I have also had to learn and work with ROS and Linux to assist with writing firmware for sensor testing & integration.
