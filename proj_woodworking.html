Woodworking

Woodworking is one of my favorite hobbies. Most of my skills have been learned from working on building projects with my dad or learning from books and online resources. Designing and building furniture is the area of woodworking I enjoy the most. It allows me to harness my creativity in the design process while also giving hands work during the build. Please see some of the Woodworking projects I have done over the years.

Bowling Lane Projects

I was able to reclaim two five and half foot sections of a maple bowling lane from demolition of an old bowling alley. These pieces were in rough shape after all the years of wear and the demolition process. The reclaimed sections were split in half to more manageable pieces to resurface into useable tabletops.

I have designed and made plans for a woodworking bench and coffee table to make use of the bowling lane table tops. The woodworking bench features a rolling base and will mount a wood vice flush to the table top. The coffee Table will feature a spider leg design similar to coffee table I have made in the past.

Currently I have made the two woodworking benches and am in the process of making the coffee tables.

Furniture from wood salvaged from neighbors renovation

During our neighbors extensive renovation to their house, I was able to salvage some discarded dimensional lumber in addition to some old Douglas Fir beams. From this wood I was able work on my joinery technique when making furniture. From this wood I was able to make a Bed Frame, Bedside table, Coffee table, Media stand and a Hanging Lamp stand.

Greenland Paddle

I was able to blend my two passions of woodworking and paddling together when I designed and crafted a traditional West greenland paddle. I followed a great guide by Chuck Holst which outlines build process from material selection to taking your own measurements to make the paddle custom length for yourself.
