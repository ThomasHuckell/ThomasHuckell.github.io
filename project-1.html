Project Overview

Legged robots are complex systems with a high degree of freedom which make optimal control methodolgies for the whole body dynamics computationally complex, especially for planning over long time horizions. Reduced order models encapsulate the whole body dynamics into lower degree of freedom representation more suitible. My research extended the existing reduced order models to include all availible standing balance strategies:

    ankle strategy: modulate center of pressure within the base of support.
    hip strategy: generate angular momentum about center of mass to produce restoring shear force at base of support.
    variable height strategy: use verticle acceleration of center of mass to momentarly increase ground reaction force magnitude.

With this unified model we examined how access to all balancing strategies impacts push recovery performance.
